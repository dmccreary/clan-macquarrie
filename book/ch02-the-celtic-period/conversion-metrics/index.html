    MacQuarrie History - Chronological Timeline

    Extracted from Chapters I & II

    Ancient Period (Pre-Christian Era)

    Before 503 AD - Celtic peoples migrate to Ireland from continental Europe - Ireland becomes known as "Scotia" - Celts develop advanced culture in northeastern Ireland - Druids practice their religion throughout the region

    Early Christian & Dalriadic Period

    390 AD - St. Ninian comes to Scotland from Rome - Preaches to the Picts - Builds small church (later replaced by Abbey)

    503 AD - Fergus mac Ere leads Scots from Ireland to Scotland - Brothers Loarn and Angus accompany him - Found Kingdom of Dalriada in Argyll - Three kingdoms now in Britain: Northern Picts, Southern Picts, and Dalriada

    521 AD - St. Columba born in Donegal, Ireland - Family: Father Phelin mac Fergus, Mother Eithne - Descended from King Niall of the 9 Hostages - Grand-nephew of Fergus mac Ere

    563 AD - St. Columba arrives in Scotland from Ireland - Given Isle of Iona (then called "I") by Dalriadic king - Establishes monastery, church, and training school

    573 AD - Battle of Ardderyd - Christian victory - St. Kentigern (Mungo) returns to Glasgow

    584 AD - St. Columba preaches near river Tay - Probable meeting with St. Kentigern

    597 AD - Death of St. Columba on Iona - Same year St. Augustine lands in southern England - Celtic Church continues his work for 600 years

    Viking Period

    6th-7th Centuries - Irish monasteries become famous for learning throughout Europe - Celtic missionaries spread across Scotland, England, and western Europe

    795 AD - First Viking raid on Iona

    801 AD - Second Viking raid on Iona

    802 AD - Third Viking raid on Iona

    806 AD - Fourth Viking raid on Iona

    8th-10th Centuries - Norse Vikings rampage throughout northern waters - Establish control of Hebrides, Orkney, and Shetland - Create stronghold in Dublin, Ireland - Scots sometimes ally with English against Norse - Scots sometimes ally with Norse against English

    Formation of Scotland

    836 AD - Alpin (son of King Achaius and Princess Ferfkusia) killed - Son Kenneth inherits Dalriadan crown and claim to Pictish throne

    843 AD - Kenneth mac Alpin claims throne of the Picts - Unites Picts and Scots by force - Capital established first at Dunstaffnage, then moved to Scone

    843-859 AD - Reign of Kenneth mac Alpin - First King of united Scotland - Stone of Destiny (Lia Fail) placed at Scone - Picts gradually lose identity, adopt Gaelic language - Beginning of Alpinian line

    850 AD (approximate) - Guaire, descendant of Kenneth MacAlpin - Progenitor of Clan MacQuarrie - Brother Finguine (Fingon) - progenitor of Clan MacKinnon

    859 AD - Death of Kenneth mac Alpin - First King of united Scotland buried at Iona - Over next 300 years, 48 Scottish Kings buried there

    859-863 AD - Donald II reigns (Kenneth's brother)

    863-881 AD - Constantine reigns (Kenneth's son)

    870 AD - Fifth Viking raid on Iona - Abbot and 86 monks slain on Martyrs' Bay

    881-882 AD - Heth reigns (Kenneth's son)

    Later Alpinian Kings

    Following Kings (dim in history) - Donald the Ruddy - Hugh the Fair - Duff the Brown - Kenneth the Grim - At least 12 of 16 kings after Kenneth assassinated or killed in battle

    970-994 AD - Kenneth III reigns

    986 AD - Sixth (and final recorded) Viking raid on Iona

    994-995 AD - Constantine reigns

    995-1003 AD - Kenneth IV reigns

    1003-1033 AD - Malcolm II reigns

    After 1033 - Duncan succeeds Malcolm II - Later killed by MacBeth

    MacQuarrie Clan Development

    Early 900s (if Farquharson tradition correct) - Clan MacQuarrie traces origin to Guaire, son of Kenneth MacAlpin

    Late 1200s (if Alan Macquarrie theory correct) - Cormac mac Airbertaich (descendant of Dalriadic rulers) - Sons: Guaire and Finguine (Fingon) - Guaire founds Clan MacQuarrie - Finguine founds Clan MacKinnon

    12th or 15th Century (disputed) - MacDonald sennachie records banquet - Chiefs seated by rank and station - MacQuarrie seated 2nd - Praised as "ancient thane" - "the brave, the fearless, the great feated"

    Centuries before 18th Century - MacQuarries firmly established on Isle of Ulva - Also hold Staffa, Little Colonsay, and part of Mull - Known as rough, turbulent fighters - Allied first with MacDonalds, later with MacLeans of Duart

    Related Clans from Alpine Line

    All descended from the Alpinian/Alpine race: - Clan MacQuarrie (from Guaire) - Clan MacKinnon (from Finguine/Fingon) - Clan MacGregor - Clan MacNaughton - Clan MacPhie - Clan Grant - Clan Macnab - Clan MacAulay

    Later Medieval Period

    1200 AD - Roman Christianity predominates over Celtic tradition - Celtic organizational structure largely replaced

    1249 AD - Reference to Cormac Mhor (MacQuarrie) - Killed in battle against Norse - Sons Alan and Gregor flee to Ireland

    1263 AD - King Alexander III defeats Norse at Largs - Claims Western Isles for Scotland - Alan (son of Cormac Mhor) returns from Ireland - Becomes MacQuarrie chief - Gregor stays in Ireland

    1285 AD - Death of Alexander III - End of Alpinian line (25 kings total from 834-1285)

    1296 AD - King Edward I of England takes Stone of Destiny - Removed from Scone to Westminster Abbey in England

    Clan Symbol & Identity

    Emblem: Pine tree (variety best able to resist strong gale)

    Crest: Dagger held aloft, ready to strike

    War Cry (Gaelic): An far m breac deag - Translation: "Here come the red-spattered tartans"

    Motto (Latin): Turris Fortis Mihi Deus - Translation: "God is a strong tower to me"

    Name Variations: - MacQuarrie (most common) - MacGuarie - Macquaire - MacGuidhre (ancient Gaelic form)

    Reputation: "The most acquisitive raiders who ever purloined barley" - "bad soldiers, but good plunderers" (meaning unwilling to submit to military discipline, not poor fighters)

    Modern Period Events (Brief)

    1688 - Fire destroys most MacQuarrie family papers - Leaves clan annals "more dubious than they might otherwise have been"

    1715 & 1745 - Jacobite uprisings - English impose repressive measures on clans - Highlanders forbidden to carry arms, wear kilts, play bagpipes

    1747 - Hereditary jurisdictions abolished - Chiefs lose legal position as magistrates - Status as heads of clans continues

    1715-1818 - Lachlan MacQuarrie - Last hereditary Chief of Ulva - Lived to age 103

    1761-1824 - Major General Lachlan Macquarie - Governor of New South Wales, Australia - Cousin of Chief Lachlan (not father and son) - Known as "Father of Australia"

    1773 - James Boswell and Dr. Samuel Johnson visit Ulva - Chief Lachlan is their "genial host" - Described as "intelligent, polite and much a man of the world"

    1777 - Isle of Ulva sold at public auction - Pays off long-standing mortgages of Chief Lachlan - End of MacQuarrie ownership after centuries

    1782 - Ban on Highland dress lifted (after 36 years)

    Post-1777 - Many MacQuarries remain on Ulva as tenants - Others emigrate to Canada, United States - Later migrations to New South Wales and Far East - Clan scatters across the globe

    Geographic Reference

    Primary MacQuarrie Territories: - Isle of Ulva (main homeland) - Isle of Staffa - Little Colonsay - Part of Isle of Mull

    Related Locations: - Iona (spiritual center) - Argyll (Dalriada) - Scone (Scottish capital) - Kilninian on Mull (parish church)

    Name Origin: "Ulva" means "wolves" in Norse
